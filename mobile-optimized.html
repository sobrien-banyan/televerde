“Both my parents were addicts. My mom was in Rockville prison. My dad was deep into his addiction and allowed me to quit school in fifth grade. I was basically out there with no guidance at all. I had to fend for myself to eat. I turned to using and selling drugs as a child just to make my way in life.”

Amanda D.

“I am a product of generational incarceration and addiction. Growing up, my mom was either in prison or actively battling addiction on the streets. I never got to know her as a person until I got older. I was raised by my dad who was also battling addiction and he was abusive.”

Amanda D.

“Before I got to prison, I lost myself tremendously. A part of my upbringing was I grew up in church, believing in God. Along the way, getting involved with bad influences caused me to lose sight of myself and my worth.”

Briana H.

“I was very fortunate that both of my parents loved me very much. Even though they weren’t together. My ex-stepfather molested me as a child and he was removed from my life by my choice when I was 18 and I moved to Arizona to get out of that situation.”

Danielle C.

“My son just recently told me, ‘You have no idea how proud I am of you and how much I look up to you.’ Coming from a kid that I didn’t raise and whom I haven’t seen since he was 4 years old is everything.”

Dominique P.

“I don’t think it hit me until I told my mom. When I called her and told her I got 17 years, I could hear her sobbing. That’s when I realized what was going on. I was going away, and I didn’t know when I’d see her again. I was alone on the longest most scary journey of my life. I did that.”

Jaclyn E.

“When I first came to prison it was a crazy experience because you don’t know what to expect. You come in and you’re scared, you’re broken, you’re lost, you’re confused… just hopeless. You don’t expect anything good to come out of it. It seems like it’s the end of the road when you walk through the gates.”

Jada V.

“There is not a moment in my day when I question my safety and my surroundings. I love the people I live with no matter their background. They’ve overcome their obstacles and that’s what we came here for.”

Kimberly M.

“I have an addiction to methamphetamine. I spent 12 years in an abusive relationship. I was a single parent. When I went to prison the first time, my daughter was 3 and my son was 15 months.”

Marla D.
